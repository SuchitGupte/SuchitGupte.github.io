About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science and Engineering at The Ohio State University, advised by Prof. Mohammad Mahdi Khalili. My research focuses on interpretability and fairness in machine learning, aiming to make AI systems more transparent, reliable, and socially responsible. Previously, I worked with Prof. John Paparrizos on projects at the intersection of data management and explainable AI. I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science from Rutgers, The State University of New Jersey. During that time, I interned with Prof. Eshed Ohn-Bar at Boston University, focusing on robust vehicle keypoint detection under heavy occlusion and truncation. I am broadly interested in understanding how complex models, particularly deep neural networks, represent, process, and reason about information. The goal of my PhD research is to combine mechanistic interpretability with algorithmic fairness to uncover the internal principles governing model behavior, and to design learning methods that are both interpretable by humans and aligned with societal values. I hope my work will contribute to building AI systems that humans can trust, understand, and effectively collaborate with in high stakes domains such as healthcare and decision support.
